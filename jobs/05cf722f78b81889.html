Job Description

Job Description:
GAF, founded in 1886, is a $3 billion company with 3,200
employees and is a leader in the building materials
industry. As America's largest roofing manufacturer, GAF
has built a strong and solid product and customer
franchise driven by continuous product innovation and
through finding ways to better serve and connect with
its customers. The residential roofing line is sold
under the GAF brand and commercial roofing is sold under
the GAF brand. The company also has other offerings in
high quality products including railings, ductwork, and
specialty fabrics. GAF has sustained a strong financial
and operating performance over the past several years,
driven from its leading capabilities, strong culture,
and a business that is only partly dependent on economic
cycles in the construction industry.
Labeled the best in quality and most-recommended by our
customers, GAF believes that employees remain our
greatest strength and best competitive advantage. Build
your future as we build the American dream one roof at a
time.
We are looking for a Shipping Supervisor for our shingle
manufacturing plant in Minneapolis, Minnesota who will
be directly responsible for supervising the
shipping/warehouse operation, and personnel. This
position will be responsible for achieving excellence in
executing and controlling assigned shipping/warehouse
operations. Ensure the Standard Operating Procedures
(SOP's) are being adhered to in the handling and storage
of all materials; i.e. finished goods or raw materials
and equipment. The Shipping Supervisor will provide the
required training and certifications to the lift truck
operators and will also be responsible for the general
housekeeping of the warehouse, assigning tasks as needed
to maintain a safe, neat and orderly environment.
The scope of this position includes direct supervision
of the shipping personnel and management of safety,
daily operations, equipment maintenance, scheduling,
daily inventory space, quality standards, and
miscellaneous supply items for operating efficiently.
This position will have overall responsibility for the
shipping/warehouse supervision function.
Specific responsibilities include, but are not limited

to:
  • Plan and direct daily and weekly activities of the
shipping/warehouse function to meet or exceed safety
goals, service metrics, shipping/warehouse budget
compliance, transportation lane compliance and
service reliability targets.
  • Oversee inbound and outbound shipping activities;
monitor performance of operators against standard
operating procedures, identify areas for improvement
and implement resolution. Handle all customer
service compliance in regard to shipment, damage and
error.
  • Mange labor; schedule shipping personnel work hours
to minimize operating costs while meeting the needs
of the customers and carriers. Make labor
assignments fairly and without prejudice to
effectively handle volumes and meet the handling
metrics within the rules of the collective
bargaining agreement. Ensure accurate usage of the
WMS system in shipping, receiving and all product
movement.
  • Manage inventory; oversee the day to day warehouse
operation and inventory control to ensure there is
sufficient daily production space, product is
accurately counted, properly stored, properly
rotated (FIFO), cycle counts are complete and
accurate. Evaluate and resolve root causes for
inventory discrepancies.
  • Provide proper training to shipping employees in the
area of safety, standards operating procedures, and
quality standards. Create a safe work environ men by
developing safe work practices and enforcing
compliance with company/site safety policies., OSHA
or other regulatory agencies. Ensures good
housekeeping and provides excellent leadership in
the safety area.
  • Generate reports or other written correspondence
needed to insure proper administration of the
department. Ensure that the following records are
properly maintained, accurately summarized and
forwarded to the appropriate parties in a timely
manner.
  • Ensure all pre and post-shift equipment inspections
are conducted. Identify and schedule equipment
maintenance needs when required. Monitor forklift
usage and recommend adjustments to maximize
utilization. Investigate unreported damage.
  • Perform other duties as assigned.
Job Requirements:
A Bachelors Degree with a minimum of Five years of
experience in a high volume distribution environment. In
addition, computer skills must exceed basic knowledge to
include Microsoft Office and Inventory Software. APICS
certification preferred.

including 401(k), medical, dental, vision, prescription
drug, tuition reimbursement, and a vacation package. At
GAF, we believe our employees are our greatest resource.
