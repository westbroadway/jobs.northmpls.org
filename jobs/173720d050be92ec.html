Labeled the best in quality and most-recommended by our

The Transportation Planner IIâ€™s responsibilities include
working with and supporting Corporate
Transportation/Logistics and the Plant Logistics Manager
in developing a distribution strategy to support their
plant site. This includes identification of potential
distribution points within remote markets. They will be
responsible for building and maintaining the carrier
base, including Private Fleet where applicable, to
support all of these shipping sites, across all
transportation modes. This carrier base will need to
meet the cost, service, and volume requirements of the
plant and the customers it serves. This includes ongoing
communications, performance reviews, and negotiations
with the established carriers, as well as prospecting
for new carriers and new transportation modes that add
value and strengthen the established base. He/she is
also responsible for managing all load tendering
function activities for their assigned plant and
affiliated warehouses, in a manner that ensures cost
effective transportation of finished goods and inbound
raw materials at service levels that meet corporate and
plant objectives.
The Transportation Planner II will use computer
applications to determine shipping requirements, measure
carrier performance, manage shipment reliability,
perform cost/service analyses, and coordinate activities
related to shipment scheduling through delivery.
Specific responsibilities include, but are not limited

to:
  • Ensure that all orders are dispatched and assigned for
pickup on the appropriate ship date to meet promised
delivery dates, within approved traffic lane pricing
guidelines.
  • Negotiate rates and truck service with motor carriers
doing business with GAFMC, for assigned Plants within
approved traffic lane pricing guidelines.
  • Work with the Dock Scheduling Coordinator and Carriers
to ensure that all shipments are scheduled and picked up
on a daily basis within dock schedule guidelines to meet
daily manpower deployment objectives for the Plant.
  • For those Plants that deploy private fleet equipment,
ensure that fleet trucks assigned to the Plant are
dispatched and deployed in a cost effective manner,
under established private fleet deployment and pricing
guidelines.
  • With assistance from Corporate Transportation and the
Logistics Manager, develop and maintain a carrier base
to support the planned service sales territories and
shipment volume. Actively participate in efforts to
expand the carrier base to meet demand spikes caused by
sales promotions, seasonal buys, weather related
business spikes, etc. This includes ongoing
communications, and negotiation, with the established
carriers as well as prospecting for new carriers that
add value and strengthen the established base.
  • Manage Shipment Demand through daily review of order
intake against Plant and carrier capacities using
established "Ship Cap" processes.
  • Partner with the Logistics Coordinator to ensure that
Customer Service, IUT planners and Sales are provided
with accurate and timely information regarding late
shipments, late deliveries, missed pickups, rescheduled
orders, expedited orders etc. In addition, provide
appropriate levels of preparation and follow up to
ensure Job Site (Time Sensitive) deliveries are
delivered as promised.
  • Partner with the Logistics Coordinator to ensure that
carrier equipment is loaded to maximum capacity under
established "vary item" process, including working with
carriers to ensure that the equipment provided meets
established order weights.
  • Ensure that all shipping paperwork (Bill of Landing,
Picking Slip, etc.) is generated on a timely basis,
distributed accurately, contains accurate information
(including tarp instructions) and is appropriately
retained for audit purposes.
  • Identify and implement process improvements that
reduce cost and improve customer service.
  • Utilize computer applications to determine shipping
requirements, measure carrier performance, manage
shipment reliability, perform cost/service analysis, and
coordinate activities related to shipment scheduling
through delivery.
  • Perform other duties as assigned.
