Job Description

GAF, founded in 1886, is a $3 billion company with 3,200 employees and is a leader in the building materials industry. As America's largest roofing manufacturer, GAF has built a strong and solid product and customer franchise driven by continuous product innovation and through finding ways to better serve and connect with its customers. The company also has other offerings in high quality products including decking, railings, decorative stone, duct work, and specialty fabrics. GAF has sustained a strong financial and operating performance over the past several years, driven from its leading capabilities, strong culture, and a business that is only partly dependent on economic cycles in the construction industry.

Labeled the best in quality and most-recommended by our customers, GAF believes that employees remain our greatest strength and best competitive advantage. Build your future as we build the American dream one roof at a time.
  • The Chemical Process/Boiler House Manager is primarily responsible for the operation of the primary raw material processing segment of the manufacturing operation along with the plant steam boiler operation. The manager would focus on process improvement and the reduction of process variation. Working through the boiler house department supervisor and boiler and stills team, the Chemical Process/Boiler House Manager would develop and maintain comprehensive procedures, policies and systems to improve safety, training, quality and efficiency.
  • Develops and manages oxidation operations equipment integrity programs, (tanks piping, hot oil systems, etc.).
  • Coordinates asphalt qualification trial plans and provides plant support for the qualification process including trials.
  • Identifies and provides guidance for implementing oxidation operations best practices.
  • Provides leadership for identified oxidation operations process improvement activities.
  • Assures raw material specifications meet the business needs.
  • Generates oxidation operations quality reports, monitors performance and provides follow-up appropriate to assure quality standards are met.
  • Drives measurement variation reduction associated with asphalt test procedures.
The Chemical Process/Boiler House Manager is also responsible for maintaining excellence in developing, executing and controlling assigned projects to achieve site goals. Through effective application of engineering expertise, the individual will conduct analysis and investigations pertaining to construction of new equipment and processes, and alteration or modification of existing facilities to optimize productive capability of the plant. Project recommendations are made taking into consideration such factors as cost, structure, maintainability, facility of production and availability of materials and equipment with emphasis on safety and optimum performance.

Specific duties include, but are not limited to:
  • Management of the oxidation operation and boilers as outlined above.
  • Focus on the design and/or improvement of electrical and mechanical systems that manage, protect and improve our plant equipment and processes.
  • Manage electrical and mechanical and process projects from conception to completion, utilizing both in-house maintenance personnel and outside contractors.
  • Reports to the Plant Manager.
